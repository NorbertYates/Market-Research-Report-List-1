<p><p>Pulse-tube cryocoolers are devices used for cooling various applications to very low temperatures. Single-stage cryocoolers provide cooling down to temperatures above 10 Kelvin (K), while two-stage cryocoolers offer cooling down to temperatures below 10 K, typically between 4 K and 10 K. Single-stage cryocoolers are simpler, smaller, and cheaper than two-stage cryocoolers, but they have higher cooling temperatures. Two-stage cryocoolers, on the other hand, are more complex, larger, and expensive, but they achieve lower cooling temperatures necessary for more demanding applications like superconductivity and space technology.</p></p>
