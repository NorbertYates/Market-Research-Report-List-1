<p><p>Radiation detection plays a crucial role in industrial and scientific settings. Geiger Counters, widely used devices, detect radiation by counting the number of ionizing events occurring in a specific time frame. Scintillation Detectors utilize crystals that emit light when struck by radiation, and the intensity of the light is measured. Solid State Detectors consist of semiconductors able to convert radiation into electrical signals for detection. These three types of detectors are commonly employed in industries dealing with radiation levels, such as nuclear power plants, medical facilities, and research institutions.</p></p>
