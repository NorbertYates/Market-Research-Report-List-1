<p><p>Carbonyl Iron Powder is a type of iron powder produced by thermal decomposition of iron pentacarbonyl, resulting in fine particles with high purity and high iron content. It is widely used in various industries, including electronics, energy storage, and automotive, due to its excellent magnetic and electrical properties. </p><p>Atomized Ultra Fine Iron Powder, on the other hand, is produced by atomization of molten iron, resulting in ultra-fine particles with high purity and uniform size distribution. It is valued for its unique characteristics, such as high surface area, good sinterability, and excellent magnetic properties.</p><p>The "Others" category in the market refers to iron powders that are not produced through the carbonyl or atomization processes, but through alternative methods like reduction or precipitation. These powders may have varying characteristics and applications, depending on their manufacturing process.</p></p>
